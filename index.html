Overview of VR, AR, and the Metaverse

They are actually very different, and the difference between them lies in the degree of immersion

  • Virtual Reality (VR)：creates a fully immersive digital environment that replaces the real world, typically experienced through headsets, allowing users to interact within a simulated space.
  • Augmented Reality (AR)：enhances the real world by overlaying digital elements, such as images or data, onto it, accessible via devices like smartphones or AR glasses.
  • The Metaverse：combines aspects of VR and AR into a persistent, shared virtual space where people can socialize, work, and play, forming an interconnected digital universe that blends physical and virtual experiences.

Evolution Of Banking Technology

  • 1397: Medici Bank pioneers modern banking with branches.
  • 1840s: The Telegraph enables faster bank communication.
  • 1950: The first universal credit card (Diners Club) was introduced.
  • 1967: First ATM installed by Barclays.
  • 1973: SWIFT network launches for global payments.
  • 1994: Internet banking begins (Stanford Federal Credit Union).
  • 2009: Bitcoin introduces blockchain and cryptocurrency.
  • 2014: Apple Pay advances mobile payments with biometrics.

The reason why VR/Metaverse matters for banking

  • Customer experience: VR/Metaverse creates immersive virtual banking environments, offering personalized, interactive services like financial planning or loan consultations, enhancing engagement and accessibility 24/7.
  • Efficiency: Virtual branches reduce physical infrastructure costs, while VR training simulations improve staff skills quickly, streamlining operations and customer service.
  • Innovation: Enables novel features like virtual product demos, blockchain-based transactions, and digital asset integration, positioning banks as forward-thinking in a digital economy.
